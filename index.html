r = [n·ΣXY − ΣX·ΣY] ÷ √([n·ΣX² − (ΣX)²] × [n·ΣY² − (ΣY)²])
Karl Pearson's r measures linear association between two continuous variables. It ranges from −1 (perfect negative) to +1 (perfect positive). A value ≥ 0.90 in a 20-observation study indicates an extremely strong and statistically significant relationship.

Spearman Rank Correlation — Context
Spearman's ρ (rho) converts values to ranks before measuring association. Unlike Pearson r, it does not assume normality or linear relationships — it tests monotonic association.
ρ = 1 − [6·Σd²] ÷ [n(n²−1)] where d = rank difference
